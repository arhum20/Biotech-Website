Biotech

Biotechnology is the use of living organisms, cells, or biological systems to create or modify products or processes for practical use.

  • Medical treatments and therapies, such as gene therapy and regenerative medicine
  • Agriculture and crop improvement, such as genetic modification to increase yield and resistance to pests and diseases.
  • Genetic testing and research, such as identifying genetic disorders and developing personalized medicine.

Bioinformatics

Bioinformatics is defined as the application of tools of computation and analysis to the capture of biological data. This means genome sequencing various living things to analyze the closeness of the relations. Genome sequence data of hundreds of creatures are available to the public in databases like BLAST: Basic Local Alignment Search Tool, which allows users to compare genome sequences. This information isn’t exclusive to the field of biology as it is beneficial to the area of medicine in diagnosing and treating diseases by identifying associations between genomes and certain diseases. Cancer care greatly benefits from this technology as cancer cells typically have many mutations or changes in the nucleotide code compared to a patient’s normal cells. Doctors can order sequencing of a patient’s tumor cells and compare them to their normal cells and to the tumor cells of other patients to pinpoint the mutations that are causing cells to grow uncontrollably and provide the best-suited treatment.
