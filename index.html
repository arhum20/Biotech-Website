Biotech

Biotechnology is the use of living organisms, cells, or biological systems to create or modify products or processes for practical use.

  • Medical treatments and therapies, such as gene therapy and regenerative medicine
  • Agriculture and crop improvement, such as genetic modification to increase yield and resistance to pests and diseases.
  • Genetic testing and research, such as identifying genetic disorders and developing personalized medicine.

Medical Robots

In 2000, the da Vinci system became the first robotic surgical platform commercially available in the US to be cleared by the FDA for use in abdominal surgery.

These robots are controlled by a surgeon and provide greater precision and control than traditional methods.

Bioinformatics

Bioinformatics is the application of computational techniques to the management and analysis of biological data, such as DNA sequences or protein structures. It involves the development of algorithms, statistical models, and software tools to analyze and interpret biological data, and can be used in areas such as gene finding, drug discovery, and evolutionary biology. Some common bioinformatics tasks include sequence alignment, gene annotation, and genome assembly. The field is interdisciplinary and draws on knowledge from computer science, biology, statistics, and mathematics.
